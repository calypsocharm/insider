## THE STORY

### A poker deck you can play, read, and count.

Calypso Star is a single 52-card deck that does three things at once. You can deal a hand of poker with it. You can read it as a complete Lenormand. And you can count its points for a yes/no answer. One box. Three ways in.

It is not three decks stitched together. It is one deck with three layers sharing the same cards — because classic Lenormand already lives inside a poker deck. The 36 traditional Lenormand symbols sit on their original cards; the 16 cards Lenormand left out become a new "minor" tier of small ocean symbols; and the twelve court cards are oceanic gods.

### The three layers

**Poker.** Four suits, Ace through King, pips and indices visible. It shuffles, it deals, it plays. Run a hand of cards and read the same hand as fate.

**Lenormand.** The 36 classic symbols, on their traditional cards, retold in tides, reefs, currents, and shore. Read it in pairs, lines, or a full Grand Tableau — exactly as Lenormand does. Only the art is retold.

**The Gods.** The J, Q, and K of each suit are oceanic gods drawn from the Calypso Star archetypes. Each suit-realm is a family: a Herald, a Guardian, a Sovereign. When a god appears in a spread, it governs the cards of its realm near it. And for fun, the gods wander — they cameo, small and half-hidden, through the Lenormand scenes. Spot-the-god is an extra layer for readers who notice; everyone else still gets a clean Lenormand card.

### The numbers tell fortunes

Every pip carries a rank meaning, modulated by its suit. The suit sets the stage; the number sets the beat. So a 7 of Hearts is "reflection in the realm of love." A 5 of Spades is "disruption in the realm of trial." Learn the spine once and you can read any number in the deck without a book.

### A definitive answer: the count

Ask a yes/no question. Draw five cards. Each card carries a value — +1, +2, −1, −2, or 0 — printed right on it. Sum them. The deck answers.

- +4 or higher: **Yes.**
- +1 to +3: **Probably yes.**
- 0: **Unclear — not yet decided.**
- −1 to −3: **Probably no.**
- −4 or lower: **No.**

No reversals by default — Lenormand traditionally doesn't use them, and the value is printed plainly on each card. (Optional reversal rules live in the guidebook for readers who want them.) It is a game, a ritual, and a reading — all from the same hand you'd play poker with.

### The world

Dark-fantasy oceanic. Abyss ground, bioluminescent teal and coral, gold line-work, wave and curl motifs, the feel of an old field-guide of the deep. The four suits are four realms: Hearts = Tides, Spades = Currents, Diamonds = Reefs, Clubs = Shores. Same Calypso Star world as the oracle and the Tarot of the Sea — the vibe, the palette, the channeler carry over.

You would be among the very first, helping shape a 52-card Lenormand before it exists and getting first access the moment it launches. No inflated numbers here. Just the chance to be in at the start.

- **Printing.** Quoting with PrintNinja, MakePlayingCards, and overseas factories; the deck will print with a linen finish on black-core stock. Foil/holo reserved for the Collector tier and stretch goals to protect the base deck margin.
